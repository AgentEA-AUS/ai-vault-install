How the AI Vault actually works

A plain map of where your knowledge lives, how your AI reaches it, and exactly what we change on your computer — which is far less than people assume.

1 · Where the vault lives

It's a folder of files. There are three copies, and they stay identical.

Nothing exotic — your business knowledge is plain text notes in a normal folder you can open, read, and back up. Each machine has its own full copy. Change one, and the others match within seconds.

No cloud in the middle. The copies talk directly to each other, encrypted. Your notes are never uploaded to Google, Dropbox, or anyone else's servers — and never posted on the internet. The server keeps 30 days of history, so a bad edit is always recoverable.

2 · What's inside the vault

Three layers, and one always wins.

Separating them is what stops the AI mixing a confirmed fact with a guess. When two notes disagree, the AI is told which one to trust.

Canonical/ What you have personally confirmed is true. Overrides everything below. 1 note
wiki/ The AI's tidy write-ups, built from your source material. 102 notes
raw/ Your original SOPs, exactly as they came in. Never edited. 110 notes

Canonical beats wiki  ·  wiki beats raw

3 · How your AI reaches it

Yes — it's installed as an MCP.

MCP is simply the standard way an AI app is allowed to use an outside tool. We install a small program called the connector. Your AI app starts it, and it reads the vault folder on your machine. That's the whole chain.

Everything happens on your own computer. The connector doesn't reach the internet — it just opens files in a folder you own. Your AI app is the only thing that ever talks to the outside world, exactly as it did before.

4 · What we change on your computer

One entry, in one settings file, per app.

This is the part people expect to be invasive. It isn't. We add a single item to your AI app's own list of tools — the same list it already keeps — and we back the file up before we touch it.

Claude's settings file  ·  %APPDATA%\Claude\claude_desktop_config.json
{ "mcpServers": { "anything-you-already-had": { ... } ← untouched "business_vault": { ← the one line we add "command": "…\AI-Vault\bin\vault-connector.exe", "args": ["C:\Users\you\AI-Vault"] } } }

What we add

  • One entry in claude_desktop_config.json
  • One entry in .codex/config.toml (only if you use Codex)
  • The connector program, in your own app folder
  • A small background app that keeps the vault in sync

What we never touch

  • Your CLAUDE.md or AGENTS.md
  • Any global instructions or system prompt
  • No skills, plugins, or extensions installed
  • Any other AI tools you'd already set up

5 · Where the "training" comes from

The rules live inside the vault — not in your settings.

This is the clever bit. Since we never edit your global files, the AI learns how to behave from two places we control, and both travel with the vault:

  • The connector introduces itself. The moment your AI starts it, it hands over its house rules.
  • The vault carries its own instruction note. The AI reads it before answering, using the vault_guide tool.

The rules the AI is given, every time:

  • Search the vault before answering from memory.
  • Say which note every fact came from.
  • If the vault has no answer, say so — never invent one.
  • Confirmed facts beat write-ups; write-ups beat raw source.
  • Never change the confirmed facts or the original SOPs.
Why that matters to you: when we improve how the AI behaves, we edit one note inside the vault. It syncs to every person on the team within seconds. Nobody reinstalls anything, ever.

6 · What the AI can actually do

Five tools. It can read everything, and add — but never destroy.

vault_guide
Read the house rules and see how the vault is laid out. Always called first.
search_vault
Find the notes that answer the question.
read_note
Open one note in full.
list_folder
Browse the folders, like Explorer.
write_note
Create a new note, or add to an existing one. It cannot overwrite or delete anything, and it's locked out of your confirmed facts and original SOPs.
